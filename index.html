Ocho is an experimental typeface. It is an exploration of the process of designing symbols within strict geometric rules.

The name "Ocho" references the foundation of the geometric rules: the 8-pixel grid. Ocho's horizontal and vertical lines are rectangles aligned exactly to the grid. Diagonal lines are rectangles rotated 45-degrees; resultant corners are corrected optically. Four weights correspond to the 8-pixel units: 8, 16, 24 and 32.

Ocho is not designed for versatility, accessibility, or readability. It's for techno DJs, space exploration projects, and forward-thinking football clubs.

Ocho is unfinished and is not available as a font. There are no uppercase glyphs. Diacritics are limited. Sometimes the 'e' looks like a 'c'. There are no italics, no ligatures. Punctuation is limited. There's no ampersand.
